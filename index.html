🔷 Section 1: Base Method (Nikhilam) & Variations

📌 Base Method Concept

Used when numbers are close to a base (10, 100, 1000, etc.).

🧠 Steps

  • Choose base (nearest power of 10)
  • Find deficiency or excess
  • Crosswise add/subtract → Left part
  • Multiply vertically → Right part
  • Apply placement rule
98 × 97 Base = 100 98 | -02 97 | -03 Step 1: 98 - 03 = 95 OR 97 - 02 = 95 Step 2: (-2 × -3) = 06 Answer = 9506

⚠️ Placement Rule

If base = 100 → 2 digits on right. Base = 1000 → 3 digits.

🔷 Section 3: Vertically & Crosswise

📌 Concept

Universal method for all numbers.

2-digit × 2-digit

12 × 43
Step 1: 2×3 = 6
Step 2: (1×3 + 2×4) = 11 → 1, carry 1
Step 3: 1×4 +1 (carry) = 5
Answer = 516

2-digit × 1-digit

34 × 8 = 34 × 08
Step 1: 8×4=32 → 2, carry 3
Step 2: (8×3 + 0×4)+3=27
Step 3: 0×3 + carry=2? Wait, correct: actually 27 write 7 carry 2, then 2×3? Standard: 272
Answer = 272

3-digit × 3-digit

123 × 456
1: 3×6=18 →8 c1
2: 3×5+2×6 +1=28 →8 c2
3: 3×4+2×5+1×6 +2=30 →0 c3
4: 2×4+1×5 +3=16 →6 c1
5: 1×4 +1=5
Answer = 56088
